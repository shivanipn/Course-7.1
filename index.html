What is Robotics?

Robotics is a branch of engineering focused on the design, development, and use of robots. The term "robot" comes from the Slavic word robota, meaning "forced labour", and was first used by Czech writer Karel Čapek in 1920.

A robot is a machine capable of sensing its environment and performing tasks either independently or with human guidance. Robots are commonly used in industries and are now becoming part of everyday life—like the Roomba, an autonomous vacuum cleaner.
There are two main types of robots:

  • Autonomous robots – Operate without human input.
  • Non-autonomous robots – Require human instructions to function.

The Laws of Robotics

While designing and building robots, we must keep in mind the three Laws of Robotics. These laws were written by Isaac Asimov in the The three laws are:

First Law

A robot may not harm a human or allow a human to come to harm through inaction.

Second Law

A robot must obey human commands unless they conflict with the First Law.

Third Law

A robot must protect its own existence, as long as it does not conflict with the First or Second Law.

The types of Robotics

Robots are used for all sorts of tasks. They are mostg, stressful or harmful to humans, and in environments which are not suitable for humans.
Some of the different types of robots based on their applications are listed below.

Industrial Robots

Used in manufacturing, assembly lines, and automation for tasks like welding, painting, and packaging.

Agricultural Robots

Used for seeding, harvesting, weed control, and monitoring soil and environment conditions.

Medical Robots

Used for surgeries, rehabilitation, patient monitoring, and providing companionship.

Military Robots

Used for tasks such as transport, surveillance, bomb disposal, search and attack operations.

Components of Robotics

The field of robotics consists of three major components - Mechanics, Electronics, and Software.

Mechanics

It deals with the moving parts as well as the physical construction of a robot.

Electronics

It deals with those parts of a robot which involve electricity. These parts make up the "nervous system" of a robot.

Software

It deals with the instructions that enable a robot to make its own decisions in the next few lessons, we will be taking a look at the electronics side of robotics.
