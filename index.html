About Electronic Components

Electronic circuits are made up of standard components such as resistors, diodes, capacitors, and integrated circuits, connected by wires or printed metal paths inside electronic devices.

Breadboard

A breadboard is a key tool used for prototyping and testing electronic circuits without soldering. It has small holes where you can easily insert wires and components.

  • Outer rows are connected vertically.
  • Middle rows are connected horizontally.
  • The gap in the center separates connections; to bridge it, you use jumper wires.

It‚Äôs ideal for building temporary circuits, such as connecting an LED and a resistor, to test ideas before finalizing a design.

LED (Light Emitting Diode)

An LED is a small electronic component that lights up when current flows through it. It's often used to indicate power, status, or operation in a circuit.

  • Available in many colors like red, green, blue, etc.
  • RGB LEDs combine red, green, and blue in one unit to produce a range of colors.
  • LEDs are used in 7-segment displays, decorative lights, and even image displays when grouped in arrays.
Knowledge Block
  • Current flows only one way in an LED.
  • A resistor must be connected in series with an LED to prevent it from burning out due to excess current.

üîπ Inductors

An inductor is a coil of wire, sometimes wound around a metal core. It stores energy as a magnetic field and resists changes in current.

  • When current flows steadily, the inductor does nothing.
  • A changing current induces voltage.
  • A simple experiment: Create an electromagnet by wrapping wire around a nail and connecting it to a battery.
üîπ Capacitor

A capacitor stores electrical energy in the form of an electric field. Its value is measured in Farads (F), usually in smaller units like pF, nF, or ¬µF.

  • Used for time-delay effects (e.g., blinking lights).
  • Helps stabilize voltage in circuits.
  • Comes in two types:
  • Polarized: has positive (+) and negative (‚àí) pins (e.g., electrolytic).
  • Non-polarized: can be connected either way (e.g., ceramic disc).

üîπ Integrated Circuit (IC)

üîπ Integrated Circuit (IC)

An Integrated Circuit (IC) is a small chip that contains many electronic components like resistors, transistors, and diodes all packed together.

  • Found in almost all modern electronics like phones, laptops, TVs, etc.
  • Each pin on an IC serves a different function.
  • ICs come in various shapes and sizes.
üí° Example IC

The 555 Timer is a popular IC used in circuits to:

  • Blink LEDs
  • Create countdowns
  • Generate clock pulses
  • Produce sound
